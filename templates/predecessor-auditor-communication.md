# Predecessor Auditor Communication Template

## Communications Between Predecessor and Successor Auditors

### Professional Standards Reference

This communication template complies with:
- AU-C Section 210, *Terms of Engagement*
- AU-C Section 510, *Opening Balances—Initial Audit Engagements, Including Reaudit Engagements*
- AU-C Section 900, *Audits of Financial Statements of Employee Benefit Plans Subject to ERISA*

---

## Overview

When a change in auditors occurs, professional standards require the successor auditor to communicate with the predecessor auditor. This process involves:

1. **Successor auditor** requests permission from client to contact predecessor
2. **Client** authorizes predecessor to respond to successor inquiries
3. **Successor auditor** sends inquiry letter to predecessor
4. **Predecessor auditor** responds to specific inquiries

## Client Authorization Letter

### Template for Client to Authorize Predecessor Response

```
[PLAN SPONSOR LETTERHEAD]


[Date]


[Predecessor Audit Firm Name]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]


Re: Authorization to Communicate with Successor Auditor
    [PLAN NAME]


Dear [Predecessor Partner Name]:

We have engaged [SUCCESSOR FIRM NAME] to audit the financial statements
of the [PLAN NAME] for the year ended December 31, [YEAR].

This letter authorizes you to respond fully to inquiries from
[SUCCESSOR FIRM NAME] concerning your audit of the Plan's financial
statements for the year ended December 31, [PRIOR YEAR], and to allow
them to review your working papers related to the Plan audit.

Please provide [SUCCESSOR FIRM NAME] with any information they request
in connection with their audit of the Plan. This authorization includes
permission to discuss:

- Your reasons for the change in auditors (if inquired)
- Information regarding disagreements with management
- Information regarding the integrity of management
- Information regarding the quality of the Plan's accounting policies
- Any fraud, illegal acts, or violations of laws and regulations
- Internal control matters
- Any other matters you believe the successor auditor should be aware of

If you have any questions, please contact [CONTACT NAME] at [PHONE].

Thank you for your cooperation.

Sincerely,


_____________________________
[Plan Administrator Name]
[Title]


cc: [Successor Audit Firm Name]
```

## Successor Auditor Inquiry Letter

### Template for Successor to Send to Predecessor

```
[SUCCESSOR AUDIT FIRM LETTERHEAD]


[Date]


[Predecessor Audit Firm Name]
[Attention: Engagement Partner Name]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]


Re: Inquiry Regarding [PLAN NAME]


Dear [Predecessor Partner Name]:

We have been engaged to audit the financial statements of the [PLAN NAME]
(the "Plan") for the year ended December 31, [YEAR]. The Plan was
previously audited by your firm. The Plan Sponsor has authorized you to
respond to our inquiries (see attached authorization letter).

In accordance with auditing standards generally accepted in the United
States of America, we request the following information regarding your
audit of the Plan:

PART 1: GENERAL INQUIRIES
-------------------------

1. Are you aware of any information that might bear on the integrity
   of management?

2. Did you have any disagreements with management regarding:
   - Accounting principles or practices?
   - Financial statement disclosures?
   - Auditing procedures or scope?
   If yes, please describe and explain how the disagreements were resolved.

3. Were there any significant communications with management or those
   charged with governance regarding:
   - Significant deficiencies or material weaknesses in internal control?
   - Fraud or illegal acts?
   - Noncompliance with laws and regulations?
   - Plan document operational failures?

4. Are you aware of any reasons we should not accept this engagement?

5. What were the reasons for the change in auditors, to the extent
   you are aware?

PART 2: PLAN-SPECIFIC INQUIRIES
-------------------------------

6. Were there any significant findings or issues noted during your
   audit(s) of the Plan? If so, please describe.

7. Were any internal control deficiencies identified? Please describe
   any material weaknesses or significant deficiencies communicated
   to management.

8. Were there any uncorrected misstatements? If so, please provide
   a summary.

9. Were there any compliance issues identified related to:
   - ERISA requirements?
   - Internal Revenue Code requirements?
   - Plan document operational matters?
   - Prohibited transactions?

10. Did the Plan undergo any correction programs (EPCRS, VFCP, DFVCP)?
    If so, please describe.

11. Were there any issues with the certification from the custodian/
    trustee for the limited scope audit?

12. Were there any significant or unusual transactions during the
    audit period(s) that required special audit consideration?

13. Were there any going concern considerations?

14. Did you become aware of any litigation, claims, or assessments
    involving the Plan?

PART 3: WORKING PAPER ACCESS
----------------------------

15. May we arrange to review portions of your working papers related
    to the Plan audit? We are particularly interested in:
    - Lead schedules and trial balances
    - Audit planning documentation
    - Testing of contributions, distributions, and participant data
    - Internal control documentation
    - Summary of adjusting entries and uncorrected misstatements
    - Communications with management and governance
    - Certification evaluation

Please advise as to your firm's policies regarding working paper access.

PART 4: OTHER INFORMATION
-------------------------

16. Is there any other information that you believe we should be aware
    of in connection with our audit of the Plan?

17. Were there any scope limitations in your audit? If so, please describe.

18. If applicable, please provide a copy of your management letter or
    communication with those charged with governance.

We would appreciate your response at your earliest convenience. If you
have any questions, please contact [CONTACT NAME] at [PHONE] or [EMAIL].

Thank you for your cooperation.

Sincerely,


_____________________________
[Partner Name]
[Successor Audit Firm Name]


Enclosure: Client Authorization Letter
```

## Predecessor Auditor Response Letter

### Template for Predecessor Response

```
[PREDECESSOR AUDIT FIRM LETTERHEAD]


[Date]


[Successor Audit Firm Name]
[Attention: Engagement Partner Name]
[Address]
[City, State ZIP]


Re: Response to Inquiry - [PLAN NAME]


Dear [Successor Partner Name]:

We are responding to your letter dated [DATE] regarding your inquiry
about our audit of the financial statements of the [PLAN NAME] for
the year ended December 31, [PRIOR YEAR]. We have received authorization
from the Plan Sponsor to respond to your inquiries.

Our responses are as follows:

PART 1: GENERAL INQUIRIES

1. Integrity of management:
   [Respond: "We are not aware of any information that might bear
   on the integrity of management" OR describe any concerns]

2. Disagreements with management:
   [Respond: "We had no disagreements with management regarding
   accounting principles, disclosures, or audit procedures" OR
   describe any disagreements and how resolved]

3. Significant communications:
   [Describe any significant communications regarding internal
   control deficiencies, fraud, illegal acts, or noncompliance,
   OR state "None"]

4. Engagement acceptance:
   [Respond: "We are not aware of any reasons you should not accept
   this engagement" OR describe any concerns]

5. Reasons for change:
   [Respond: "We understand the change is due to [reason provided
   by client - e.g., competitive bid process, rotation policy]"
   OR "We are not aware of the specific reasons for the change"]

PART 2: PLAN-SPECIFIC INQUIRIES

6. Significant findings:
   [Describe any significant findings OR state "We noted no
   significant findings during our audit"]

7. Internal control deficiencies:
   [Describe any control deficiencies communicated OR state
   "We identified no material weaknesses or significant deficiencies"]

8. Uncorrected misstatements:
   [Describe any uncorrected misstatements OR state "There were
   no uncorrected misstatements"]

9. Compliance issues:
   [Describe any compliance issues identified OR state "We identified
   no significant compliance issues"]

10. Correction programs:
    [Describe any correction programs OR state "To our knowledge,
    the Plan did not participate in any correction programs"]

11. Certification issues:
    [Describe any issues OR state "We noted no issues with the
    certification from the custodian"]

12. Unusual transactions:
    [Describe any unusual transactions OR state "We are not aware
    of any significant unusual transactions"]

13. Going concern:
    [Describe any issues OR state "There were no going concern
    considerations"]

14. Litigation:
    [Describe any litigation OR state "We are not aware of any
    litigation, claims, or assessments involving the Plan"]

PART 3: WORKING PAPER ACCESS

15. Working paper review:
    [Respond regarding firm policy on working paper access.
    Example: "We will make available for your review, at our offices,
    portions of our working papers related to the Plan audit. Please
    contact [NAME] to schedule a mutually convenient time. Our standard
    working paper access policies require that you:
    - Sign our working paper access letter
    - Review at our offices under supervision of our personnel
    - Not copy or retain any working papers

    Alternatively, we can provide copies of [specific items] upon
    your request."]

PART 4: OTHER INFORMATION

16. Other matters:
    [Describe any other matters OR state "We are not aware of any
    other matters that should be brought to your attention"]

17. Scope limitations:
    [Describe any scope limitations OR state "There were no scope
    limitations in our audit beyond the limited scope election under
    29 CFR 2520.103-8"]

18. Management letter:
    [Respond: "Attached is a copy of our management letter dated
    [DATE]" OR "We did not issue a management letter"]

Our responses are based on our knowledge as of the date of this letter
and our records. This communication is intended solely for your use in
connection with your audit of the Plan and should not be used for any
other purpose.

If you have additional questions, please do not hesitate to contact me.

Sincerely,


_____________________________
[Partner Name]
[Predecessor Audit Firm Name]


Enclosures (if applicable):
- Copy of management letter
- Copy of governance communication
```

## Working Paper Access Letter

### Template for Working Paper Review Agreement

```
[PREDECESSOR AUDIT FIRM LETTERHEAD]


WORKING PAPER ACCESS AGREEMENT


Plan Name: [PLAN NAME]
Predecessor Firm: [PREDECESSOR FIRM NAME]
Successor Firm: [SUCCESSOR FIRM NAME]
Date of Access: [DATE]


The successor auditor agrees to the following conditions for reviewing
the predecessor auditor's working papers:

1. The working papers remain the property of [PREDECESSOR FIRM NAME].

2. The working papers will be reviewed at our offices under the
   supervision of our personnel.

3. The successor auditor will not copy, reproduce, or retain any
   working papers without express written permission.

4. The review is solely for the purpose of assisting in the audit
   of the Plan's financial statements.

5. The successor auditor acknowledges that the predecessor auditor's
   work was performed for the predecessor's own purposes and may
   not be relied upon by the successor auditor.

6. The successor auditor will maintain the confidentiality of all
   information obtained through this review.

7. Any notes or extracts made during the review will be:
   [Select one:]
   ☐ Prohibited
   ☐ Permitted with prior approval
   ☐ Permitted for the following areas only: _______________

8. The successor auditor understands that certain information may
   be withheld due to legal or regulatory requirements.


ACKNOWLEDGED AND AGREED:

Successor Auditor:

Firm: _______________________________________________________
By: _________________________________________________________
Title: ______________________________________________________
Date: _______________________________________________________


Predecessor Auditor:

Firm: _______________________________________________________
```
