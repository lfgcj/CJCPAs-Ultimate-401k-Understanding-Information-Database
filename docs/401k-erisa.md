# ERISA Requirements for 401(k) Plans

## Overview

The Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974 (ERISA) is the primary federal law governing private sector employee benefit plans, including 401(k) plans. Understanding ERISA is essential for plan sponsors, administrators, and fiduciaries.

## ERISA Background and Purpose

### History

- **Enacted**: September 2, 1974
- **Effective**: Generally January 1, 1975
- **Signed by**: President Gerald Ford
- **Primary Sponsor**: Senator Harrison Williams

### Purpose

ERISA was enacted to:
1. Protect the interests of employee benefit plan participants and beneficiaries
2. Establish standards of conduct for plan fiduciaries
3. Require disclosure and reporting to the federal government and participants
4. Establish civil enforcement remedies
5. Establish funding requirements for pension plans

### Key Events Leading to ERISA

| Year | Event |
|------|-------|
| 1963 | Studebaker plant closure - 4,000 workers lost pensions |
| 1965 | Presidential committee on corporate pension funds |
| 1972-74 | Congressional hearings and development |
| 1974 | ERISA signed into law |

### Administering Agencies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|---------------|
| Department of Labor (DOL) | Fiduciary standards, reporting, disclosure |
| Internal Revenue Service (IRS) | Tax qualification, participation, vesting, funding |
| Pension Benefit Guaranty Corporation (PBGC) | Plan termination insurance (defined benefit only) |

## Plans Covered by ERISA

### Covered Plans

ERISA generally covers:
- Private sector retirement plans
- 401(k) plans
- Pension plans
- Profit sharing plans
- Employee stock ownership plans (ESOPs)
- Health and welfare plans

### Exempt Plans

ERISA does NOT cover:
| Plan Type | Reason |
|-----------|--------|
| Governmental plans | Exempt under ERISA Section 4(b)(1) |
| Church plans | Exempt under ERISA Section 4(b)(2) |
| Plans maintained solely to comply with workers' compensation, unemployment, or disability insurance | Not employee benefit plans |
| Plans maintained outside the U.S. for nonresident aliens | Not U.S. plans |
| Unfunded excess benefit plans | Exempt under ERISA Section 4(b)(5) |
| IRAs | Not employer plans |

### 401(k) Plan Coverage

A 401(k) plan is subject to ERISA if:
- Established by an employer engaged in commerce
- For the benefit of employees
- Provides retirement income or defers income until termination

ERISA requires extensive reporting and disclosure:

## Reporting and Disclosure Requirements

### Summary Plan Description (SPD)

**Required Contents:**
| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Plan name | Official plan name |
| Sponsor information | Name, address, EIN |
| Plan administrator | Contact information |
| Plan type | 401(k), profit sharing, etc. |
| Eligibility | Requirements to participate |
| Contributions | Types and formulas |
| Vesting | Schedule and calculation |
| Distributions | When and how benefits paid |
| Claims procedures | How to file and appeal |
| ERISA rights | Statement of participant rights |

**Distribution Requirements:**
- Within 90 days of becoming participant
- Within 30 days of written request
- Updated every 5 years (if changed)
- Updated every 10 years (regardless)

### Summary of Material Modifications (SMM)

Required when plan is amended:
- Within 210 days of plan year end in which change adopted
- Must describe changes in plain language
- May be incorporated into updated SPD

### Summary Annual Report (SAR)

Annual summary of financial information:
- Within 9 months of plan year end (or 2 months after Form 5500 filing)
- Summarizes Form 5500 financial information
- Distributed to participants and beneficiaries

### Form 5500 Annual Report

Comprehensive annual report including:
- Plan information
- Financial information
- Insurance information
- Service provider information
- Audit report (large plans)
- Various schedules

**Filing Deadline:**
- 7 months after plan year end
- Up to 2.5 months extension with Form 5558

### Individual Benefit Statements

**Participant-directed plans (like 401(k)):**
- At least quarterly
- Account balance
- Investment information
- Fee information

**Other plans:**
- At least annually

### Fee Disclosures

**404a-5 Participant Disclosure:**
- Initial disclosure on eligibility
- Annual disclosure thereafter
- Quarterly fee statements
- Investment performance and fee information

**408(b)(2) Service Provider Disclosure:**
- Service providers must disclose fees to plan
- Both direct and indirect compensation
- Required before entering contract

## Minimum Standards

### Participation Standards

**ERISA Section 202:**
Maximum eligibility requirements:
- Age: 21 years
- Service: 1 year (1,000 hours)
- Entry dates: Semi-annual

Cannot exclude:
- Based solely on maximum age
- Part-time employees meeting hours

### Vesting Standards

**ERISA Section 203:**
Minimum vesting for employer contributions:

| Schedule Type | Maximum |
|---------------|---------|
| Cliff Vesting | 3 years |
| Graded Vesting | 2-6 years |

Employee contributions always 100% vested.

### Benefit Accrual Rules

**ERISA Section 204:**
For defined contribution plans:
- Allocations must follow plan formula
- Cannot reduce accrued benefit
- Certain amendments require notice

### Service Counting

**ERISA Section 202-204:**
Rules for counting service:
- Year of service = 1,000 hours in 12-month period
- Hours counting methods
- Break in service rules
- Service before age 18 or plan effective date

## Fiduciary Responsibility

### Fiduciary Status

**ERISA Section 3(21):**
A person is a fiduciary to extent they:
1. Exercise discretionary authority/control over plan management
2. Exercise authority/control over plan assets
3. Have discretionary authority over administration
4. Provide investment advice for fee

### Fiduciary Duties

**ERISA Section 404:**

| Duty | Requirement |
|------|-------------|
| Loyalty | Act solely in interest of participants |
| Prudence | Act with care, skill, prudence, diligence |
| Diversification | Diversify investments to minimize large losses |
| Plan Documents | Follow documents consistent with ERISA |

### Fiduciary Protections

**ERISA Section 404(c):**
If plan meets requirements:
- Participant must have control
- Sufficient information provided
- Adequate investment options
- Fiduciary relieved of liability for participant choices

### Co-Fiduciary Liability

**ERISA Section 405:**
Liable for co-fiduciary breach if:
- Participate knowingly in breach
- Own breach enables other breach
- Know of breach and fail to remedy

## Prohibited Transactions

### Party in Interest Transactions

**ERISA Section 406(a):**
Fiduciary cannot cause plan to transact with party in interest:
- Sale, exchange, or lease of property
- Lending of money or credit
- Furnishing goods, services, or facilities
- Transfer or use of plan assets

### Self-Dealing

**ERISA Section 406(b):**
Fiduciary cannot:
- Deal with assets in own interest
- Act on behalf of adverse party
- Receive consideration from transaction party

### Exemptions

**ERISA Section 408:**

| Exemption | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Participant loans | If meet requirements |
| Reasonable compensation | For necessary services |
| Bank deposits | Bearing reasonable interest |
| Investment advice | With proper disclosure |
| Class exemptions | DOL-granted exemptions |

## Enforcement and Penalties

### Civil Enforcement

**ERISA Section 502:**
Who can bring action:
- Participants and beneficiaries
- Secretary of Labor
- Fiduciaries
- State attorneys general (certain cases)

### Available Relief

| Relief Type | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Recovery of benefits | Enforce benefit rights |
| Breach of fiduciary duty | Personal liability |
| Injunction | Prevent violations |
| Removal of fiduciary | Remove breaching fiduciary |
| Other equitable relief | As court determines |

### Criminal Penalties

**ERISA Section 501:**
- Willful violation: Up to $100,000 fine and/or 10 years imprisonment
- Organizations: Up to $500,000 fine

### Civil Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to file Form 5500 | $250/day, up to $150,000 |
| Failure to provide documents | Up to $110/day per participant |
| Failure to provide notices | Various penalties |
| Prohibited transactions | 15% excise tax (100% if uncorrected) |

### DOL Enforcement Tools

- Investigation and audit
- Voluntary compliance programs
- Civil litigation
- Criminal referral
- Consent orders

## Plan Amendments and Termination

### Amendment Requirements

**ERISA Section 402:**
Plan must provide amendment procedure:
- Identify who can amend
- Specify process
- Document amendments

**Restrictions:**
- Cannot reduce accrued benefits (Section 204(g))
- Must provide Section 204(h) notice for certain reductions
- Anti-cutback rules apply

### Plan Termination

**ERISA Section 403:**
Requirements for termination:
- Follow plan termination procedure
- Distribute all assets to participants
- File final Form 5500
- Participants become 100% vested

## ERISA Preemption

### Preemption Doctrine

**ERISA Section 514:**
ERISA preempts state laws that "relate to" employee benefit plans.

### Scope of Preemption

| Preempted | Not Preempted |
|-----------|---------------|
| State benefit mandates | State insurance regulation |
| State claims procedures | State criminal laws |
| State fiduciary standards | State domestic relations orders |
| State garnishment (mostly) | State tax laws (limited) |

### Significance

- Creates uniform federal regulation
- Plans don't have to comply with 50 state laws
- Participants have federal remedies
- Some limitations on state causes of action

## Key ERISA Sections for 401(k) Plans

### Quick Reference

| Section | Topic |
|---------|-------|
| Section 3 | Definitions |
| Section 101-111 | Reporting and disclosure |
| Section 201-211 | Participation and vesting |
| Section 301-308 | Funding (DB plans) |
| Section 401-414 | Fiduciary responsibility |
| Section 501-515 | Administration and enforcement |

### Important Definitions

**ERISA Section 3:**
- Participant: Anyone eligible or participating
- Beneficiary: Person entitled to receive benefit
- Fiduciary: Person with discretionary authority
- Party in interest: Related parties (sponsors, fiduciaries, service providers)
- Plan assets: Assets of the plan
