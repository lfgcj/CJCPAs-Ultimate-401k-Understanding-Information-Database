# IRS Requirements for 401(k) Plans

## Overview

The Internal Revenue Service (IRS) is responsible for ensuring that 401(k) plans meet the qualification requirements of the Internal Revenue Code (IRC). Meeting these requirements allows the plan to receive favorable tax treatment. This comprehensive guide covers all major IRS requirements for maintaining a qualified 401(k) plan.

## Qualification Requirements Overview

### Tax-Qualified Status Benefits

A qualified plan provides significant tax benefits:

| Benefit | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Employer deduction | Contributions are tax-deductible |
| Tax-deferred growth | Participants don't pay current tax on earnings |
| Pre-tax contributions | Employee deferrals reduce taxable income |
| Rollover eligibility | Tax-free transfers between qualified plans |
| FICA savings | Deferrals exempt from FICA (employer) |

### Key IRC Sections

| Section | Topic |
|---------|-------|
| IRC 401(a) | General qualification requirements |
| IRC 401(k) | Cash or deferred arrangements |
| IRC 401(m) | Matching and employee contributions |
| IRC 402 | Taxation of distributions |
| IRC 410 | Eligibility and participation |
| IRC 411 | Vesting |
| IRC 414 | Definitions and special rules |
| IRC 415 | Contribution and benefit limits |
| IRC 416 | Top-heavy rules |

### Consequences of Disqualification

| Consequence | Effect |
|-------------|--------|
| Loss of deduction | Employer cannot deduct contributions |
| Immediate taxation | Participants taxed on vested benefits |
| Trust taxation | Trust becomes taxable entity |
| Distribution penalties | May face early withdrawal penalties |
| ERISA violations | Potential DOL issues |

## Eligibility and Participation Rules

### IRC Section 410(a) Requirements

#### Maximum Age Requirement

- Plan cannot require participant to be older than 21
- Plan may use younger age
- Cannot exclude based on attaining maximum age

#### Maximum Service Requirement

- Cannot require more than 1 year of service
- Year of service = 1,000 hours in 12-month computation period
- May use 2 years if 100% immediate vesting

#### Entry Date Requirements

Must enter plan no later than:
- First day of plan year after meeting requirements, OR
- 6 months after meeting requirements
- Whichever is earlier

### Computation Periods

| Period | Definition |
|--------|------------|
| Initial period | 12 months from hire date |
| Subsequent periods | Plan years, or shifting 12-month periods |
| Hours requirement | 1,000 hours in computation period |

### Service Calculation Methods

| Method | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Actual hours | Count actual hours worked |
| Equivalencies | Days worked × factor (e.g., 10 hours/day) |
| Elapsed time | Treat each day as credited |

### Excludable Employees

Plans may exclude:

| Category | Requirement |
|----------|-------------|
| Union employees | Retirement benefits subject to good faith bargaining |
| Nonresident aliens | No U.S. source income |
| Part-time employees | With care for coverage requirements |
| Certain classifications | If coverage tests satisfied |

### Long-Term Part-Time Employees (SECURE 2.0)

Effective 2025, must allow deferrals for employees with:
- 500+ hours for 2 consecutive years (reduced from 3 years)
- May exclude from employer contributions
- May exclude from testing

## Contribution Limits

### IRC Section 402(g) - Elective Deferrals

Maximum employee deferral limit:

| Year | Limit | Catch-Up (50+) |
|------|-------|----------------|
| 2024 | $23,000 | $7,500 |
| 2023 | $22,500 | $7,500 |
| 2022 | $20,500 | $6,500 |

### IRC Section 415(c) - Annual Additions

Maximum annual additions (all sources combined):

| Year | Limit | Or 100% Compensation |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| 2024 | $69,000 | Lesser of |
| 2023 | $66,000 | Lesser of |

#### Annual Additions Include

| Included | Not Included |
|----------|--------------|
| Employee deferrals | Catch-up contributions |
| Employer contributions | Rollovers |
| Forfeitures allocated | Loan repayments |
| After-tax contributions | Restorative payments |

### IRC Section 401(a)(17) - Compensation Limit

Maximum compensation considered:

| Year | Limit |
|------|-------|
| 2024 | $345,000 |
| 2023 | $330,000 |

### Catch-Up Contributions

For participants age 50 or older:

| Year | Amount |
|------|--------|
| 2024 | $7,500 |
| 2023 | $7,500 |

SECURE 2.0 enhanced catch-up (ages 60-63):
- Effective 2025
- $10,000 or 150% of regular catch-up
- Whichever is greater

### Excess Contribution Corrections

| Situation | Correction |
|-----------|------------|
| Excess 402(g) | Return by April 15 of following year |
| Excess 415 | Return employee contributions, reduce employer |
| Excess ADP/ACP | Corrective distribution or QNEC |

## Vesting Requirements

### IRC Section 411 Standards

#### Minimum Vesting Schedules

For employer contributions:

| Schedule | Years |
|----------|-------|
| 3-year cliff | 0% until 3 years, then 100% |
| 6-year graded | 20% after 2 years, 20% per year |

#### Example Vesting Schedules

**3-Year Cliff:**
| Years | Vested % |
|-------|----------|
| 0-2 | 0% |
| 3+ | 100% |

**6-Year Graded:**
| Years | Vested % |
|-------|----------|
| 0-1 | 0% |
| 2 | 20% |
| 3 | 40% |
| 4 | 60% |
| 5 | 80% |
| 6+ | 100% |

### Always 100% Vested

| Contribution Type | Reason |
|-------------------|--------|
| Employee deferrals (pre-tax) | IRC requirement |
| Employee deferrals (Roth) | IRC requirement |
| Rollover contributions | Participant's own money |
| Safe harbor contributions | Safe harbor requirement |
| QNEC/QMAC | Testing requirement |

### Vesting Service Counting

#### Hour of Service

Hour for which employee is:
- Paid or entitled to pay
- For performance of duties
- For periods without duties (vacation, sick, etc.)

#### Elapsed Time Alternative

Credit all service from hire to termination without counting hours.

#### Break in Service Rules

| Break Type | Effect |
|------------|--------|
| 1-year break | Period of 500 or fewer hours |
| 5-year break | Non-vested service may be disregarded |
| Parental leave | Up to 501 hours credited |
| Military leave | USERRA protections apply |

### Forfeitures

When participant terminates without full vesting:

| Plan Option | Use |
|-------------|-----|
| Reduce contributions | Offset employer contribution |
| Allocate to participants | Pro-rata allocation |
| Pay expenses | Reasonable plan expenses |

Timing: Per plan document, typically earlier of:
- Distribution of vested balance
- 5 consecutive 1-year breaks

## Nondiscrimination Requirements

### ADP Test (IRC 401(k)(3))

Tests employee deferrals:

| NHCE ADP | Maximum HCE ADP |
|----------|-----------------|
| 0% - 2% | 2 × NHCE |
| 2% - 8% | NHCE + 2% |
| Over 8% | 1.25 × NHCE |

### ACP Test (IRC 401(m)(2))

Tests matching and after-tax contributions:
- Same limits as ADP
- Tested separately

### IRC Section 410(b) Coverage

Plans must benefit broad group of employees:

**Ratio Percentage Test:**
```
NHCE benefiting % / HCE benefiting % ≥ 70%
```

### IRC Section 401(a)(4) Nondiscrimination

Plans cannot discriminate in favor of HCEs:
- Contributions/benefits must be nondiscriminatory
- Benefits, rights, features must be available
- Plan amendments must not discriminate

### Top-Heavy Rules (IRC 416)

If key employees hold >60% of assets:
- 3% minimum contribution for non-key employees
- Accelerated vesting required

### HCE Definition (IRC 414(q))

Highly Compensated Employee if:
- 5%+ owner (current or prior year), OR
- Compensation >$155,000 (2024) in prior year

### Key Employee Definition (IRC 416)

For top-heavy purposes:
- Officers with compensation >$220,000 (2024)
- 5% owners
- 1% owners with compensation >$150,000

## Distribution Rules

### IRC Section 401(a)(9) - When Distributions Required

#### Required Beginning Date

Generally, April 1 following later of:
- Year participant turns 73 (SECURE 2.0)
- Year participant retires (non-5% owners)

**Age Requirements (RMD):**
| Birth Year | RMD Age |
|------------|---------|
| Before 1951 | 72 |
| 1951-1959 | 73 |
| 1960+ | 75 (effective 2033) |

### Distribution Triggering Events

Distributions permitted upon:

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| Termination | After separation from service |
| Death | To beneficiary |
| Disability | IRS definition applies |
| Hardship | If permitted by plan |
| Age 59½ | If permitted by plan |
| Plan termination | Full distribution required |

### Hardship Distributions (IRC 401(k)(2)(B))

#### Safe Harbor Reasons

| Reason | Description |
|--------|-------------|
| Medical expenses | Unreimbursed medical |
| Home purchase | Principal residence |
| Tuition | Post-secondary education |
| Eviction prevention | Prevent eviction/foreclosure |
| Funeral expenses | Burial/funeral costs |
| Casualty loss | Primary residence damage |
| FEMA disaster | Disaster area expenses |

#### Requirements (Post-2019)

- Need cannot be reasonably satisfied elsewhere
- Distribution limited to amount of need
- No repayment required
- No 6-month suspension required

### Rollovers (IRC 402(c))

#### Eligible Rollover Distributions

Most distributions are eligible except:
- Required minimum distributions
- Hardship distributions
- Substantially equal periodic payments
- Corrective distributions

#### Direct Rollover Option

Plan must offer direct rollover to:
- Another qualified plan
- IRA
- 403(b) plan
- Governmental 457(b)

#### Mandatory 20% Withholding

If participant receives distribution (not direct rollover):
- 20% federal withholding required
- Participant has 60 days to complete rollover

## Plan Document Requirements

### Written Plan Requirement

Plan must be in writing and include:

| Element | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Eligibility | How employees become eligible |
| Contributions | Types and formulas |
| Vesting | Schedule and calculation |
| Distributions | When and how paid |
| Administration | How plan operates |
| Amendment | How plan can be changed |
| Termination | Process for ending plan |

### Plan Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Pre-approved | IRS opinion/advisory letter |
| Individually designed | Custom document |
| Prototype | Standardized with limited options |
| Volume submitter | More flexibility than prototype |

### Amendment Requirements

Plans must be amended for:
- Law changes (by applicable deadline)
- Desired plan changes
- Correction of errors

### Determination Letter

Optional IRS ruling on qualified status:
- Available for individually designed plans
- Pre-approved plans have IRS letter
- Provides reliance on qualified status

### Plan Document Restatement

Pre-approved plans must be restated:
- On IRS's restatement cycle
- Currently every 6 years
- Maintain qualified status

## Required Minimum Distributions

### General Rules

#### Required Beginning Date (RBD)

| Participant | RBD |
|-------------|-----|
| 5%+ owner | April 1 after year reaching RMD age |
| All others | April 1 after later of RMD age or retirement |

#### Annual Distribution Requirement

After RBD, distribution required each year by December 31.

### Calculating RMDs

#### Lifetime RMDs

```
RMD = Account Balance (12/31 prior year) / Life Expectancy Factor
```

Use Uniform Lifetime Table unless:
- Sole beneficiary is spouse
- Spouse is more than 10 years younger
- Then use Joint Life Table

#### Uniform Lifetime Table (Partial)

| Age | Factor |
|-----|--------|
| 73 | 26.5 |
| 74 | 25.5 |
| 75 | 24.6 |
| 80 | 20.2 |
| 85 | 16.0 |
| 90 | 12.2 |

### Post-Death RMDs

| Beneficiary Type | Distribution Period |
|------------------|---------------------|
| Eligible designated beneficiary | Life expectancy |
| Designated beneficiary | 10-year rule |
| Non-designated beneficiary | 5-year rule |

#### Eligible Designated Beneficiaries

- Surviving spouse
- Minor child (of participant)
- Disabled individual
- Chronically ill individual
- Individual not more than 10 years younger

### Roth 401(k) RMDs

SECURE 2.0 change (effective 2024):
- Roth 401(k) exempt from RMDs during owner's lifetime
- Matches Roth IRA treatment

### RMD Penalties

| Situation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Failure to take RMD | 25% of shortfall |
| Timely correction | Reduced to 10% |

## Correction Programs (EPCRS)

### Overview

Employee Plans Compliance Resolution System allows correction of plan errors:

| Program | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| SCP | Self-Correction Program |
| VCP | Voluntary Correction Program |
| Audit CAP | Audit Closing Agreement Program |

### Self-Correction Program (SCP)

#### Eligibility

- Plan must have determination letter or use pre-approved document
- Error must be operational (not document)
- Correction must be timely

#### Types of Errors

| Error Type | Correction Period |
|------------|-------------------|
| Insignificant | Any time |
| Significant | By end of 2nd plan year after error |

#### SCP Corrections

No IRS submission required:
- Identify error
- Determine correction method
- Make correction
- Document correction

### Voluntary Correction Program (VCP)

#### When to Use

- Document failures
- Significant operational failures beyond SCP window
- Demographic failures
- Desire IRS approval

#### VCP Process

```
1. Identify error
2. Determine correction
3. Prepare VCP submission
4. Pay user fee
5. Submit to IRS
6. IRS review
7. Compliance statement issued
8. Implement correction
```

#### VCP User Fees (2024)

| Plan Assets | Fee |
|-------------|-----|
| <$500,000 | $1,500 |
| $500,000-$10M | $3,000 |
| >$10M | $3,500 |

### Audit CAP

When error discovered on IRS audit:
- Negotiate correction with IRS
- Pay sanction
- Implement correction
- Higher penalties than VCP

### Common Corrections

| Error | Correction |
|-------|------------|
| Missed deferral | QNEC equal to missed deferral + earnings |
| Late deposit | Deposit with lost earnings |
| Incorrect match | Corrective contribution or refund |
| Coverage failure | QNEC to excluded employees |
| Document failure | Retroactive amendment if possible |

## Recent Legislative Changes

### SECURE 2.0 Act (2022)

| Provision | Effective |
|-----------|-----------|
| RMD age to 73 | 2023 |
| RMD age to 75 | 2033 |
| Roth 401(k) RMD exemption | 2024 |
| Enhanced catch-up (60-63) | 2025 |
| Mandatory auto-enrollment | 2025 |
| Student loan matching | 2024 |
| Emergency savings | 2024 |
| Part-time eligibility | 2025 |
| Starter 401(k) | 2024 |

### Key Changes Summary

#### Automatic Enrollment

New plans (post-2024) must:
- Auto-enroll at 3%+ default rate
- Auto-escalate 1% annually to 10%+
- Exempt: Small employers, church plans, governmental

#### Roth Catch-Up

Effective 2026 (delayed):
- Catch-up contributions must be Roth
- For participants earning >$145,000 (prior year FICA wages)
- Note: This is a separate threshold from HCE determination ($155,000)
- Pre-tax still available for others

#### Student Loan Matching

Employers may:
- Match student loan payments
- As if participant had deferred
- Effective 2024

#### Emergency Savings Accounts

Plans may offer:
- Linked emergency savings
- Up to $2,500 account
- Penalty-free withdrawal
- Effective 2024
