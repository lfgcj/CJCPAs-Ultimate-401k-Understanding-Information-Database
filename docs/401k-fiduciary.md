# Fiduciary Responsibilities in 401(k) Plans

## Overview

Fiduciary responsibility is one of the most important aspects of 401(k) plan management. Fiduciaries are held to the highest standard of care under the law and can be personally liable for breaches of their duties. This comprehensive guide covers all aspects of fiduciary responsibility.

## What is a Fiduciary?

### ERISA Definition

Under ERISA Section 3(21), a person is a fiduciary to the extent they:

1. **Exercise discretionary authority or control** over plan management
2. **Exercise authority or control** over plan assets
3. **Have discretionary authority or responsibility** in plan administration
4. **Provide investment advice for a fee** or other compensation

### Functional Test

Fiduciary status is determined by function, not title:
- Actions taken determine status
- No requirement to be named fiduciary
- Can be fiduciary for some purposes, not others
- Part-time or limited fiduciary status possible

### Named vs Functional Fiduciaries

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Named Fiduciary | Identified in plan document |
| Functional Fiduciary | Becomes fiduciary by actions |
| Plan Administrator | Named in plan (often employer) |
| Trustee | Named in trust agreement |

## Fiduciary Duties Under ERISA

### The Four Core Duties

#### 1. Duty of Loyalty (Exclusive Benefit Rule)

**ERISA Section 404(a)(1)(A)**

"A fiduciary shall discharge his duties with respect to a plan solely in the interest of the participants and beneficiaries and for the exclusive purpose of providing benefits to participants and their beneficiaries and defraying reasonable expenses of administering the plan."

**Requirements:**
- Act solely in participant interest
- Cannot favor employer over participants
- Avoid conflicts of interest
- Cannot use plan assets for own benefit

**Examples of Violations:**
- Using plan assets to benefit the company
- Selecting investments based on personal benefit
- Excessive compensation to service providers
- Self-dealing transactions

#### 2. Duty of Prudence (Prudent Expert Rule)

**ERISA Section 404(a)(1)(B)**

"With the care, skill, prudence, and diligence under the circumstances then prevailing that a prudent man acting in a like capacity and familiar with such matters would use."

**Requirements:**
- Higher than ordinary prudent person standard
- Must act as someone familiar with such matters
- Process matters as much as outcomes
- Document decision-making process

**The Prudent Process:**
1. Identify the decision to be made
2. Gather relevant information
3. Consider alternatives
4. Document analysis
5. Make informed decision
6. Monitor and review

#### 3. Duty to Diversify

**ERISA Section 404(a)(1)(C)**

"By diversifying the investments of the plan so as to minimize the risk of large losses, unless under the circumstances it is clearly prudent not to do so."

**Requirements:**
- Diversify plan investments
- Spread risk among different investments
- Consider correlation of investments
- Exception only if clearly prudent

**Diversification Considerations:**
- Asset class diversification
- Investment style diversification
- Manager diversification (for active)
- Geographic diversification

#### 4. Duty to Follow Plan Documents

**ERISA Section 404(a)(1)(D)**

"In accordance with the documents and instruments governing the plan insofar as such documents and instruments are consistent with the provisions of [ERISA]."

**Requirements:**
- Follow plan document terms
- Operate per trust agreement
- Honor investment policy statement
- Exception: Cannot follow if conflicts with ERISA

## Types of Fiduciaries

### Plan Sponsor

**Role:**
- Establishes and maintains plan
- Makes plan design decisions
- Often named as Plan Administrator

**Fiduciary Decisions:**
- Plan design choices
- Amendment decisions
- Provider selection (settlor function debate)

### Plan Administrator

**Role:**
- Day-to-day plan operations
- Named in plan document
- Signs Form 5500

**Fiduciary Decisions:**
- Eligibility determinations
- Benefit calculations
- Claims and appeals

### Named Fiduciary

**Role:**
- Identified in plan document
- Authority to control and manage
- May delegate duties

**Fiduciary Decisions:**
- As specified in plan document
- Retained duties not delegated

### Investment Committee

**Role:**
- Oversight of investment options
- Select and monitor investments
- Establish investment policy

**Fiduciary Decisions:**
- Investment selection
- Investment monitoring
- Fund changes
- Manager selection

### Trustee

**Role:**
- Holds plan assets
- Manages investments (if directed)
- Follows plan document

**Types:**

| Type | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| Directed Trustee | Follows instructions; limited liability |
| Discretionary Trustee | Investment discretion; full fiduciary |

### Investment Advisor/Manager

**Role:**
- Provides investment advice
- May manage assets directly

**Types:**

| Type | Responsibilities |
|------|-----------------|
| 3(21) Advisor | Provides advice; shares fiduciary duty |
| 3(38) Manager | Discretion over investments; assumes fiduciary duty |

## Fiduciary Standards of Conduct

### Exclusive Benefit Standard

All fiduciary actions must be:
- For participants and beneficiaries
- To provide benefits or pay expenses
- Not for employer benefit

### Prudent Expert Standard

Must demonstrate:
- Knowledge appropriate to the task
- Diligence in gathering information
- Careful analysis of alternatives
- Reasoned decision-making

### Document Everything

| What to Document | Why |
|-----------------|-----|
| Meeting minutes | Shows deliberation |
| Analysis performed | Shows prudent process |
| Alternatives considered | Shows diligence |
| Decisions and rationale | Shows reasoned judgment |
| Monitoring activities | Shows ongoing oversight |

## Investment Fiduciary Responsibilities

### Investment Selection

#### Process Requirements

1. **Establish Investment Policy Statement (IPS)**
   - Investment objectives
   - Asset allocation guidelines
   - Selection criteria
   - Monitoring procedures

2. **Evaluate Investment Options**
   - Performance history
   - Risk characteristics
   - Fees and expenses
   - Manager experience

3. **Consider Participant Needs**
   - Range of risk tolerance
   - Investment time horizons
   - Financial sophistication
   - Diversification needs

4. **Document Selection Process**
   - Criteria used
   - Options considered
   - Reasons for selection/rejection

### Investment Monitoring

#### Ongoing Responsibilities

| Frequency | Activity |
|-----------|----------|
| Quarterly | Review performance |
| Annually | Compare to benchmarks |
| Annually | Fee benchmarking |
| As needed | Manager changes |
| As needed | Market developments |

#### Watch List Criteria

Consider placing on watch list if:
- Underperformance vs benchmark
- Peer ranking decline
- Manager turnover
- Style drift
- Organizational issues

### QDIA Selection

For plans with automatic enrollment:
- Select appropriate QDIA
- Must be among approved QDIA types
- Monitor ongoing appropriateness
- Provide required notices

### Fee Monitoring

Fiduciary duty includes:
- Understanding all plan fees
- Ensuring fees are reasonable
- Benchmarking against comparables
- Negotiating when appropriate

## Administrative Fiduciary Responsibilities

### Participant Communications

Ensure participants receive:
- Summary Plan Description
- Summary of Material Modifications
- Summary Annual Report
- Fee disclosures (404a-5)
- QDIA notices
- Blackout notices (if applicable)

### Claims Processing

Requirements:
- Follow claims procedures
- Timely decisions
- Written explanations for denials
- Full and fair review process
- Proper appeals handling

### Contributions Management

Responsibilities:
- Timely deposit of contributions
- Accurate calculation
- Proper allocation
- Compliance with limits

### Recordkeeping Oversight

Monitor service providers for:
- Accurate recordkeeping
- Timely processing
- Proper valuations
- Security of data

## Prohibited Transactions

### ERISA Section 406 Prohibitions

#### Transactions with Parties in Interest

A fiduciary shall not cause the plan to engage in a transaction with a party in interest that constitutes:

| Prohibition | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Sale/exchange | Selling or exchanging property |
| Lending | Lending money or extending credit |
| Furnishing goods/services | Providing goods or services |
| Transfer of assets | Transferring plan assets |
| Use of assets | Using assets for party's benefit |

#### Parties in Interest

Include:
- Fiduciaries
- Counsel to the plan
- Plan employees
- Service providers
- Plan sponsor and affiliates
- 10% or more shareholders
- Unions covering plan participants

### Self-Dealing Prohibitions

A fiduciary shall not:
- Deal with plan assets for own interest
- Act on behalf of adverse party
- Receive consideration from transaction party

### Exemptions

#### Statutory Exemptions

| Exemption | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Loans to participants | If meet IRC Section 72(p) |
| Reasonable compensation | For necessary services |
| Office space/services | At reasonable rates |
| Investment advice | With proper disclosure |

#### Class Exemptions

DOL has issued class exemptions for:
- Securities transactions
- Bank collective investment funds
- Insurance company products
- Cross-trading

#### Individual Exemptions

Available through DOL application for specific transactions.

### Consequences of Prohibited Transactions

| Consequence | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Excise tax | 15% of amount involved (Section 4975) |
| Correction | Must undo transaction |
| Additional tax | 100% if not corrected |
| Personal liability | Fiduciary may be liable |

## Co-Fiduciary Liability

### When Liability Attaches

A fiduciary is liable for another fiduciary's breach if:

1. **Participation in Breach**
   - Knowingly participated in breach
   - Concealed breach
   - Failed to remedy known breach

2. **Enabling Breach**
   - Own breach enabled other breach
   - Failed to monitor co-fiduciary
   - Imprudent delegation

3. **Knowledge of Breach**
   - Knew of breach
   - Failed to make reasonable efforts to remedy

### Avoiding Co-Fiduciary Liability

- Monitor delegated functions
- Respond promptly to known issues
- Document oversight activities
- Take action when problems identified
- Establish clear responsibilities

## Fiduciary Liability and Protection

### Personal Liability

Fiduciaries can be held personally liable for:
- Losses resulting from breach
- Profits made through breach
- Other equitable relief
- Restoration of losses

### Protection Strategies

#### 1. Proper Delegation

ERISA allows delegation to:
- Investment managers (3(38))
- Other named fiduciaries
- Must be proper delegation with monitoring

#### 2. 404(c) Protection

If plan meets 404(c) requirements:
- Participants must have control
- Sufficient information provided
- Adequate investment options
- Fiduciary not liable for participant choices

#### 3. Fiduciary Liability Insurance

Types:
| Type | Coverage |
|------|----------|
| Fidelity Bond | Required; covers theft/dishonesty |
| Fiduciary Liability | Covers breach of duty; optional |
| E&O Insurance | Covers errors; for professionals |

#### 4. Indemnification

- Plan or sponsor may indemnify fiduciaries
- Cannot eliminate ERISA liability
- Can shift economic burden

### Fidelity Bond Requirements

**ERISA Section 412 requires:**
- Every fiduciary and every person who handles funds/property
- Bond of at least 10% of plan assets
- Minimum $1,000, maximum $500,000 ($1,000,000 for employer securities)

## Fiduciary Best Practices

### Governance Structure

1. **Establish Committee**
   - Formal charter
   - Clear responsibilities
   - Regular meetings
   - Meeting minutes

2. **Define Roles**
   - Who is responsible for what
   - Delegation documentation
   - Reporting relationships

3. **Document Policies**
   - Investment policy statement
   - Governance policy
   - Fee policy
   - Service provider policy

### Meeting Best Practices

| Element | Best Practice |
|---------|---------------|
| Frequency | At least quarterly |
| Agenda | Prepared in advance |
| Minutes | Detailed and timely |
| Attendance | Track and ensure quorum |
| Materials | Distribute in advance |
| Follow-up | Action items tracked |

### Investment Oversight

1. **Quarterly Review**
   - Performance vs benchmarks
   - Peer comparisons
   - Fee review
   - Manager updates

2. **Annual Deep Dive**
   - Comprehensive fee review
   - IPS review and update
   - Service provider evaluation
   - Manager due diligence

3. **Documentation**
   - Investment reports
   - Committee discussions
   - Decisions and rationale
   - Action items

### Service Provider Management

1. **Selection Process**
   - RFP process
   - Multiple bids
   - Due diligence
   - Reference checks

2. **Contract Review**
   - Services defined
   - Fees clear
   - Performance standards
   - Termination provisions

3. **Ongoing Monitoring**
   - Service quality
   - Fee reasonableness
   - Regulatory compliance
   - Industry standards

### Training and Education

Fiduciaries should:
- Understand their duties
- Stay current on regulations
- Attend relevant training
- Document education received

### Common Fiduciary Mistakes to Avoid

| Mistake | Prevention |
|---------|------------|
| Ignoring fees | Regular fee benchmarking |
| Inadequate monitoring | Establish review schedule |
| Poor documentation | Minutes for all meetings |
| Self-dealing | Conflict of interest policy |
| Failure to follow plan | Regular document review |
| Inattention | Active engagement |
