# SECURE 2.0 Act: Comprehensive Guide for 401(k) Plans

## Complete Analysis of the SECURE 2.0 Act of 2022

The SECURE 2.0 Act (Setting Every Community Up for Retirement Enhancement Act of 2022) was signed into law on December 29, 2022, as part of the Consolidated Appropriations Act, 2023. This comprehensive guide covers all provisions affecting 401(k) plans, with effective dates, audit implications, and implementation guidance.

## Overview and Background

### Legislative History

| Event | Date |
|-------|------|
| SECURE Act 1.0 signed | December 20, 2019 |
| SECURE 2.0 introduced | Various bills 2021-2022 |
| SECURE 2.0 signed into law | December 29, 2022 |
| Provisions effective | 2023 through 2027+ |

### Purpose of SECURE 2.0

SECURE 2.0 builds on the original SECURE Act with goals to:
- Expand retirement plan coverage
- Increase retirement savings
- Simplify plan administration
- Enhance retirement income options
- Modernize retirement plan rules

### Scope

The Act contains over 90 provisions affecting:
- Defined contribution plans (401(k), 403(b))
- Defined benefit plans
- IRAs
- SIMPLE and SEP plans
- Government plans
- 529 plans

**This guide focuses on provisions affecting 401(k) plans.**

## Contribution Limit Changes

### Annual Contribution Limits

| Year | Employee Deferral | Catch-up (50+) | Total (415) | Compensation |
|------|-------------------|----------------|-------------|--------------|
| 2023 | $22,500 | $7,500 | $66,000 | $330,000 |
| 2024 | $23,000 | $7,500 | $69,000 | $345,000 |
| 2025 | $23,500* | $7,500* | $70,000* | $350,000* |

*Projected, subject to IRS announcement

### Enhanced Catch-Up Contributions (Section 109)

**Effective:** Taxable years beginning after December 31, 2024

| Age | Catch-up Limit | Calculation |
|-----|----------------|-------------|
| 50-59 | Standard | Regular catch-up limit |
| 60-63 | Enhanced | Greater of $10,000 or 150% of 2024 catch-up limit (indexed) |
| 64+ | Standard | Regular catch-up limit |

**2025 Enhanced Catch-Up Calculation:**
- 2024 catch-up limit: $7,500
- 150% of $7,500 = $11,250
- Greater of $10,000 or $11,250 = **$11,250**

**Example:**
```
Participant age 62 in 2025:
  Regular deferral limit:         $23,500
  Enhanced catch-up:             +$11,250
  Total deferral:                $34,750
  vs. age 55 participant:        $31,000 ($23,500 + $7,500)
```

### Catch-Up Roth Requirement (Section 603)

**Effective:** Taxable years beginning after December 31, 2025

| Participant Type | Pre-2026 | 2026+ |
|-----------------|----------|-------|
| NHCE | Pre-tax or Roth | Pre-tax or Roth |
| HCE ($155,000+ prior year) | Pre-tax or Roth | **Roth only** |

**Key Points:**
- Applies to participants who earned $145,000+ in prior year from employer (indexed)
- Catch-up contributions by HCEs MUST be Roth
- Plans without Roth feature cannot accept HCE catch-ups
- Regular deferrals by HCEs can still be pre-tax

**Audit Implications:**
- Test HCE identification
- Verify catch-up source coding
- Test plan Roth availability

## RMD Changes

### Required Beginning Date Changes

| Birth Year | Age 72 Rule | SECURE 2.0 Age | Notes |
|------------|-------------|----------------|-------|
| Before 1951 | Age 70½ | N/A | Pre-SECURE Act |
| 1951-1959 | Age 72 | Age 73 | Effective 2023 |
| 1960 or later | N/A | Age 75 | Effective 2033 |

### RMD From Roth 401(k) Eliminated (Section 303)

**Effective:** Taxable years beginning after December 31, 2023

| Pre-2024 | 2024+ |
|----------|-------|
| Roth 401(k) had RMD requirement | No RMD required for Roth 401(k) |
| Must take RMD or roll to Roth IRA | Can leave in plan indefinitely |

### Reduced RMD Excise Tax (Section 302)

**Effective:** Taxable years beginning after December 31, 2022

| Situation | Pre-2023 | 2023+ |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Missed RMD | 50% excise tax | 25% excise tax |
| Corrected timely | 50% | 10% (if corrected in "correction window") |

**Correction Window:**
- End of second taxable year after the year of the failure
- File amended return to claim reduced rate

## Automatic Enrollment Requirements

### Mandatory Auto-Enrollment (Section 101)

**Effective:** Plan years beginning after December 31, 2024

**Applies to:** 401(k) and 403(b) plans established AFTER December 29, 2022

**Does NOT Apply to:**
- Plans established before December 29, 2022
- Employers with 10 or fewer employees
- Employers in business less than 3 years
- Church plans
- Governmental plans
- SIMPLE 401(k) plans

### Requirements

| Requirement | Standard |
|-------------|----------|
| Initial deferral rate | 3% minimum, 10% maximum |
| Annual escalation | 1% per year |
| Maximum deferral | At least 10%, no more than 15% |
| Default investment | QDIA compliant |
| Notice | 90 days before, then annually |
| Opt-out | Must allow immediate opt-out |
| Permissible withdrawals | 90-day window to withdraw |

### Example Timeline

```
New employee starts January 1, 2026:

Day 1:        Automatically enrolled at 3%
Year 2:       Escalates to 4%
Year 3:       Escalates to 5%
Year 4:       Escalates to 6%
Year 5:       Escalates to 7%
Year 6:       Escalates to 8%
Year 7:       Escalates to 9%
Year 8:       Escalates to 10%
Years 9+:     Remains at 10% (or up to 15% if plan allows)
```

### Audit Procedures

| Step | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Verify plan establishment date |
| 2 | Test initial enrollment rate (3-10%) |
| 3 | Test annual escalation implementation |
| 4 | Verify 90-day notice provided |
| 5 | Test opt-out processing |
| 6 | Verify QDIA for default investments |
| 7 | Test 90-day withdrawal processing |

## Roth Provisions

### Roth Employer Contributions (Section 604)

**Effective:** Contributions made after December 29, 2022

Plans MAY allow participants to designate employer contributions as Roth:
- Matching contributions
- Nonelective contributions
- Any employer contribution

**Tax Treatment:**
- Participant must include in income when contributed
- Not subject to 10% early withdrawal penalty
- Qualified distributions are tax-free

**Vesting:**
- Roth employer contributions must be 100% vested when made
- Cannot use graded or cliff vesting

**Audit Implications:**
- Verify Roth designation forms
- Test immediate vesting
- Verify W-2 reporting

### Roth SIMPLE and SEP (Section 601)

**Effective:** Taxable years beginning after December 31, 2022

- SIMPLE IRAs may accept Roth contributions
- SEP IRAs may accept Roth contributions
- Employer must offer both pre-tax and Roth options

## Student Loan Matching

### Matching on Student Loan Payments (Section 110)

**Effective:** Plan years beginning after December 31, 2023

Employers MAY provide matching contributions based on:
- Qualified student loan payments (QSLPs)
- Instead of or in addition to elective deferrals

**Requirements:**

| Requirement | Details |
|-------------|---------|
| Certification | Employee must certify loan payments |
| Frequency | Match computed separately, at least annually |
| Eligible loans | Qualified education loans (IRC 221(d)) |
| Matching formula | Same as deferral match |
| Nondiscrimination | Separate ACP test allowed |

**Example:**
```
Plan match: 100% of first 5% of compensation

Employee A:                    Employee B:
Salary: $50,000               Salary: $50,000
Deferral: $0                  Deferral: $2,500 (5%)
Student loan payment: $2,500  Student loan payment: $0
Match: $2,500                 Match: $2,500

Both receive same match despite different contribution types
```

### Certification Requirements

Participants must certify:
- Loan is qualified education loan
- Payment was made during the year
- Amount of payment
- Loan was for employee (or spouse/dependent)

### Audit Procedures

| Step | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Verify plan amendment adopted |
| 2 | Test certification documentation |
| 3 | Verify matching formula consistent |
| 4 | Test timing of match deposits |
| 5 | Verify ACP testing method |

## Emergency Savings Accounts

### Pension-Linked Emergency Savings Accounts (PLESA) (Section 127)

**Effective:** Plan years beginning after December 31, 2023

Plans MAY establish emergency savings accounts linked to 401(k):

**Key Features:**

| Feature | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Eligibility | Non-highly compensated employees only |
| Contribution limit | $2,500 (indexed) |
| Contribution source | After-tax Roth |
| Investment | Principal preservation vehicle |
| Withdrawals | First withdrawal per month penalty-free |
| Employer match | Treated like deferral for matching |

**Account Structure:**
- Separate short-term savings account
- Linked to 401(k) plan
- Auto-enrollment permitted
- Contributions stop when $2,500 reached
- Excess flows to Roth 401(k)

### Withdrawal Rules

| Withdrawal Type | Treatment |
|-----------------|-----------|
| First withdrawal per month | No penalty, no tax (Roth basis) |
| Additional withdrawals | Plan may charge reasonable fees |
| At termination | Transfer to Roth 401(k) or distribute |

### Audit Procedures

| Step | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Verify only NHCEs eligible |
| 2 | Test contribution limit monitoring |
| 3 | Verify investment is principal preservation |
| 4 | Test withdrawal processing |
| 5 | Verify excess transfers to Roth 401(k) |

## Long-Term Part-Time Employee Rules

### Eligibility Changes (Section 125)

**SECURE Act (2021-2024):**
- Three consecutive years of 500+ hours
- Must count service from 2021+

**SECURE 2.0 (2025+):**
- Two consecutive years of 500+ hours
- Must count service from 2023+

### Implementation Timeline

```
                    2021    2022    2023    2024    2025    2026
                    ----    ----    ----    ----    ----    ----
SECURE 1.0 Rule:    Year 1  Year 2  Year 3  ENTER
SECURE 2.0 Rule:                    Year 1  Year 2  ENTER

Under SECURE 2.0:
- Track hours starting 2023
- Employee with 500+ hours in 2023 and 2024
- Enters plan in 2025
```

### Vesting Rules for Part-Time Employees

| Rule | Application |
|------|-------------|
| Special vesting | Plans may apply separate vesting schedule |
| 12-month period | Each 12-month period counts as 1 year |
| No 1,000-hour requirement | Part-time years count for vesting |

### Audit Procedures

| Step | Procedure |
|------|-----------|
| 1 | Identify employees with 500-999 hours |
| 2 | Track consecutive years of service |
| 3 | Verify entry date calculation |
| 4 | Test vesting schedule application |
| 5 | Verify contributions for eligible part-time |

## Correction Programs and Penalties

### Enhanced EPCRS (Section 305)

**Self-Correction Expanded:**
- Plan loan failures may be self-corrected
- No significance limitation for self-correction
- Extended correction period

**Reduced VCP Fees:**
- Anonymous submission option
- Fee schedule revised

### Reduced Late Filing Penalties (Section 351)

**Form 5500 Late Filing:**
- Reasonable cause defense expanded
- First-time filer relief

### DOL Enforcement Changes

| Change | Details |
|--------|---------|
| Educational outreach | Required before assessment |
| Penalty waiver authority | Broader DOL discretion |
| Small employer relief | Enhanced for <100 employees |
