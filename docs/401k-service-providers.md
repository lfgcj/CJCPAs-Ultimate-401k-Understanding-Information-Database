## Types of Service Providers

### Recordkeeper

**Role:**
- Maintain participant accounts
- Process transactions
- Generate statements
- Provide participant services

**Services Provided:**
- Contribution processing
- Investment elections
- Distribution processing
- Loan administration
- Participant website
- Call center

### Third-Party Administrator (TPA)

**Role:**
- Plan compliance
- Document preparation
- Testing
- Government filings

**Services Provided:**
- Plan document maintenance
- Nondiscrimination testing
- Form 5500 preparation
- Eligibility calculations
- Contribution calculations
- Compliance monitoring

### Custodian/Trustee

**Role:**
- Hold plan assets
- Execute transactions
- Safeguard investments

**Types:**
| Type | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|
| Directed Trustee | Follows instructions only |
| Discretionary Trustee | Investment discretion |
| Custodian | Holds assets, no trust powers |

### Investment Advisor/Manager

**Role:**
- Provide investment advice
- May manage investments

**Types:**
| Type | ERISA Section | Responsibility |
|------|---------------|----------------|
| 3(21) Advisor | 3(21)(A)(ii) | Shares fiduciary duty |
| 3(38) Manager | 3(38) | Assumes fiduciary duty |

### Auditor

**Role:**
- Annual plan audit
- Financial statement preparation
- DOL/IRS compliance

**Requirements:**
- Independent CPA
- EBP experience
- Peer reviewed

### Other Providers

| Provider | Role |
|----------|------|
| Actuary | Defined benefit calculations |
| Attorney | Legal matters |
| Communication firm | Participant education |
| Enrollment firm | Plan enrollment services |

## Selection Process

### RFP Process

#### Step 1: Define Needs
- Identify services required
- Determine priorities
- Establish budget expectations

#### Step 2: Develop RFP
- Service requirements
- Fee format
- Data requested
- Timeline

#### Step 3: Identify Candidates
- Industry research
- Referrals
- Current providers

#### Step 4: Distribute RFP
- Send to selected candidates
- Allow adequate response time
- Answer questions uniformly

#### Step 5: Evaluate Responses
- Create scoring matrix
- Compare services
- Compare fees
- Check references

#### Step 6: Conduct Finalist Presentations
- In-depth discussion
- Clarify responses
- Meet service team

#### Step 7: Make Selection
- Committee discussion
- Document rationale
- Negotiate final terms

#### Step 8: Implement
- Contract execution
- Transition planning
- Communication

### RFP Content

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| Plan overview | Size, demographics, current setup |
| Service requirements | Specific services needed |
| Fee requirements | How to present fees |
| Questions | Specific information requested |
| Timeline | Key dates |
| Evaluation criteria | How responses will be judged |

### Selection Criteria

| Criterion | Considerations |
|-----------|---------------|
| Experience | Years in business, client base |
| Services | Scope and quality |
| Technology | Platform capabilities |
| Fees | Total cost, transparency |
| References | Client satisfaction |
| Financial stability | Long-term viability |
| Service team | Quality and responsiveness |

## Fee Benchmarking

### Types of Fees

| Fee Type | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Asset-based | Percentage of assets |
| Per-participant | Fixed amount per person |
| Flat fee | Fixed annual amount |
| Transaction fees | Per transaction charges |
| Fund expenses | Investment expense ratios |

### Benchmarking Process

1. **Identify all fees**
   - Direct fees
   - Indirect fees (revenue sharing)
   - Transaction fees
   - Investment fees

2. **Calculate total cost**
   - All fees combined
   - Per participant
   - As percentage of assets

3. **Compare to benchmarks**
   - Similar-sized plans
   - Industry surveys
   - Competitor quotes

4. **Evaluate reasonableness**
   - Services received
   - Quality of service
   - Market comparison

### Fee Benchmarking Resources

| Resource | Type |
|----------|------|
| 401k Averages Book | Annual publication |
| NEPC Survey | Investment consultant data |
| Plan Sponsor Council | Industry surveys |
| DOL lawsuit data | Fee litigation benchmarks |
| Competitive bids | Direct market comparison |

### Total Cost Analysis Example

```
Plan Assets: $50,000,000
Participants: 500

Recordkeeping (asset-based):     0.10% = $50,000
TPA (per participant):           $50   = $25,000
Investment advisory:             0.05% = $25,000
Audit:                           Flat  = $15,000
Custodian:                       0.02% = $10,000
                                        ---------
Total Direct Fees:                      $125,000

Average fund expense ratio:      0.45% = $225,000
                                        ---------
Total Plan Cost:                        $350,000

Per participant:                        $700
As % of assets:                         0.70%
```

## 408(b)(2) Disclosures

### Purpose

Service providers must disclose compensation to plan fiduciaries to help determine fee reasonableness.

### Covered Service Providers

Providers who:
- Reasonably expect $1,000+ in compensation
- Are fiduciaries, registered investment advisors, recordkeepers, brokers, or provide certain services

### Required Disclosures

| Category | Information |
|----------|-------------|
| Services | Description of services provided |
| Status | Whether provider is fiduciary |
| Compensation | All direct and indirect compensation |
| Investment | Information about investments if applicable |
| Termination | How compensation affected by termination |

### Direct Compensation

Paid directly by plan:
- Administrative fees
- Per-participant charges
- Transaction fees
- Fixed fees

### Indirect Compensation

Paid by third parties:
- Revenue sharing from funds
- Sub-TA fees
- 12b-1 fees
- Soft dollars

### Timing

- Initial disclosure before contract
- Changes within 60 days
- Annual disclosure may be required

### Fiduciary Duties

Plan fiduciary must:
- Review disclosures
- Evaluate reasonableness
- Document review
- Take action if issues identified

## Ongoing Monitoring

### Monitoring Framework

| Area | Frequency |
|------|-----------|
| Service quality | Ongoing |
| Fee review | Annually |
| Benchmarking | Every 3-5 years or as needed |
| Contract compliance | Annually |
| Financial stability | Annually |

### Service Quality Metrics

| Metric | Measurement |
|--------|-------------|
| Participant satisfaction | Surveys, complaints |
| Processing accuracy | Error rates |
| Response time | Call center metrics |
| Website uptime | Availability data |
| Report timeliness | Delivery vs deadlines |
| Issue resolution | Time to resolve |

### Annual Review Checklist

```
☐ Review service level agreements
☐ Evaluate service quality metrics
☐ Review fees vs contract
☐ Compare fees to benchmarks
☐ Review 408(b)(2) disclosure updates
☐ Assess participant satisfaction
☐ Review cybersecurity practices
☐ Evaluate financial stability
☐ Document committee review
☐ Address any issues identified
```

### Red Flags

| Red Flag | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Declining service | Address with provider |
| Fee increases | Evaluate justification |
| Staff turnover | Assess impact |
| Financial problems | Consider alternatives |
| Repeated errors | Escalate concerns |
| Cybersecurity issues | Immediate attention |

## Service Provider Agreements

### Key Contract Terms

| Term | Importance |
|------|------------|
| Scope of services | What's included |
| Fees and compensation | All costs |
| Service levels | Expected performance |
| Term and termination | Length, exit provisions |
| Liability | Responsibility allocation |
| Confidentiality | Data protection |
| ERISA compliance | Fiduciary acknowledgment |

### Service Level Agreements

| Service | Metric |
|---------|--------|
| Contribution processing | 1-2 business days |
| Distribution processing | 3-5 business days |
| Call center response | <30 seconds average |
| Website availability | 99.5%+ uptime |
| Statement delivery | Within X days of quarter |

### Negotiation Points

| Point | Consideration |
|-------|---------------|
| Fee caps | Protection from increases |
| Performance guarantees | Consequences for failures |
| Termination flexibility | Ability to exit |
| Fee credits | Revenue sharing offsets |
| Insurance requirements | Adequate coverage |

## Changing Service Providers

### Reasons to Change

| Reason | Consideration |
|--------|---------------|
| Poor service | Document issues first |
| High fees | Benchmark to confirm |
| Better options | Competitive evaluation |
| Plan growth | Outgrown current provider |
| Merger/acquisition | Consolidation needs |

### Transition Process

1. **Planning**
   - Select new provider
   - Develop timeline
   - Identify transition team

2. **Data Transfer**
   - Historical records
   - Participant data
   - Investment positions
   - Loan balances

3. **Communication**
   - Participant notification
   - Blackout notice (if required)
   - Enrollment materials

4. **Conversion**
   - Asset transfer
   - Account mapping
   - Testing and validation

5. **Post-Conversion**
   - Verify accuracy
   - Resolve issues
   - Final reconciliation

### Blackout Period Considerations

If participant rights suspended:
- 30+ days advance notice
- Written notice required
- Explain what's affected
- Expected duration
- Contact for questions
