## Components of the Audit Report

### Standard Audit Report Elements

Under SAS 136, the auditor's report must include:

#### 1. Title
Must include the word "Independent":
```
Independent Auditor's Report
```

#### 2. Addressee
Typically addressed to:
- Plan Administrator
- Plan Committee
- Plan Trustee
- Plan Sponsor

#### 3. Opinion Section

```
Opinion

We have audited the financial statements of [Plan Name], which 
comprise the statement of net assets available for benefits as 
of December 31, 20XX, and the related statement of changes in 
net assets available for benefits for the year then ended, and 
the related notes to the financial statements.

In our opinion, the accompanying financial statements present 
fairly, in all material respects, the financial position of 
[Plan Name] as of December 31, 20XX, and the changes in its 
net assets available for benefits for the year then ended in 
accordance with accounting principles generally accepted in 
the United States of America.
```

#### 4. Basis for Opinion

```
Basis for Opinion

We conducted our audit in accordance with auditing standards 
generally accepted in the United States of America (GAAS). Our 
responsibilities under those standards are further described in 
the Auditor's Responsibilities for the Audit of the Financial 
Statements section of our report. We are required to be 
independent of [Plan Name] and to meet our other ethical 
responsibilities, in accordance with the relevant ethical 
requirements relating to our audit. We believe that the audit 
evidence we have obtained is sufficient and appropriate to 
provide a basis for our audit opinion.
```

#### 5. Responsibilities of Management

```
Responsibilities of Management for the Financial Statements

Management is responsible for the preparation and fair presentation 
of the financial statements in accordance with accounting principles 
generally accepted in the United States of America, and for the 
design, implementation, and maintenance of internal control relevant 
to the preparation and fair presentation of financial statements 
that are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or 
error.

In preparing the financial statements, management is required to 
evaluate whether there are conditions or events, considered in the 
aggregate, that raise substantial doubt about [Plan Name]'s ability 
to continue as a going concern for one year after the date that the 
financial statements are available to be issued.
```

#### 6. Auditor's Responsibilities

```
Auditor's Responsibilities for the Audit of the Financial Statements

Our objectives are to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the 
financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, 
whether due to fraud or error, and to issue an auditor's report that 
includes our opinion.

[Additional paragraphs describe the audit process]
```

#### 7. Supplemental Schedules Section

```
Report on Supplemental Schedules Required by ERISA

The supplemental schedules of [assets held for investment purposes, 
reportable transactions, etc.] as of December 31, 20XX, are required 
by the Department of Labor's Rules and Regulations for Reporting and 
Disclosure under the Employee Retirement Income Security Act of 1974, 
and are not a required part of the financial statements. Such 
supplemental schedules are the responsibility of management.
```

#### 8. Signature, Date, and Location

```
[CPA Firm Name]

[City, State]
[Date of Report]
```

## Types of Audit Opinions

### Unmodified (Unqualified) Opinion

**Meaning:** Financial statements are fairly presented in all material respects.

**When Issued:**
- No material misstatements identified
- Sufficient evidence obtained
- No scope limitations
- Financial statements comply with GAAP

**Standard Language:**
"In our opinion, the financial statements present fairly, in all material respects..."

### Qualified Opinion

**Meaning:** Financial statements are fairly presented except for specific issues.

**When Issued:**
- Material but not pervasive misstatement
- Unable to obtain sufficient evidence on specific item
- Disagreement with management on accounting treatment

**Types:**
| Type | Cause |
|------|-------|
| Scope limitation | Cannot verify specific item |
| GAAP departure | Accounting not per GAAP |
| Inadequate disclosure | Required disclosure missing |

**Language:**
"In our opinion, except for [specific issue], the financial statements present fairly..."

### Adverse Opinion

**Meaning:** Financial statements are NOT fairly presented.

**When Issued:**
- Material and pervasive misstatements
- Significant departures from GAAP
- Financial statements misleading

**Language:**
"In our opinion, because of the significance of the matter discussed in the Basis for Adverse Opinion section, the financial statements do not present fairly..."

### Disclaimer of Opinion

**Meaning:** Auditor cannot form an opinion.

**When Issued:**
- Unable to obtain sufficient evidence
- Significant scope limitations
- Multiple material uncertainties
- Limited scope audit for certified investments

**Language:**
"Because of the significance of the matter described in the Basis for Disclaimer of Opinion section, we have not been able to obtain sufficient appropriate audit evidence to provide a basis for an audit opinion..."

## Limited Scope Audit Reports

### What is a Limited Scope Audit?

Under ERISA Section 103(a)(3)(C), plans can limit the audit scope for investments held by banks, trust companies, or insurance carriers that provide certification.

### Limited Scope Report Components

#### Scope Limitation Paragraph

```
As permitted by 29 CFR 2520.103-8 of the Department of Labor's 
Rules and Regulations for Reporting and Disclosure under ERISA, 
the plan administrator instructed us not to perform, and we did 
not perform, any auditing procedures with respect to the 
information summarized in Note X, which was certified by 
[Bank/Trust Company], the trustee (or custodian) of the Plan, 
except for comparing the information with the related financial 
statements and supplemental schedules. We have been informed by 
the plan administrator that the trustee (or custodian) holds the 
Plan's investment assets and executes investment transactions. 
The plan administrator has obtained a certification from the 
trustee (or custodian) as of December 31, 20XX, and for the year 
then ended, that the information provided to the plan 
administrator by the trustee (or custodian) is complete and accurate.
```

#### Disclaimer on Certified Information

```
Because of the significance of the matter described in the Basis 
for Disclaimer of Opinion section, we are unable to obtain 
sufficient appropriate audit evidence to provide a basis for an 
audit opinion on the financial statements as a whole. Accordingly, 
we do not express an opinion on these financial statements.
```

#### Opinion on Form and Content

```
Opinion on the Form and Content of Supplemental Schedules

The supplemental schedule(s) [identified] are the responsibility 
of the Plan's management. The supplemental schedule(s) has/have 
been subjected to the auditing procedures applied in our audit of 
the financial statements and, in our opinion, is/are fairly stated, 
in all material respects, in relation to the financial statements 
as a whole.
```

### Certification Requirements

The certifying institution must provide written certification that:
1. Information furnished is complete and accurate
2. Prepared in accordance with GAAP
3. Includes all required elements
4. Signed by authorized official

## Notes to Financial Statements

### Required Disclosures

| Topic | Content |
|-------|---------|
| Description of Plan | General plan description, eligibility, contributions, vesting |
| Summary of Accounting Policies | Basis of accounting, valuation methods, estimates |
| Fair Value Measurements | Hierarchy levels, valuation techniques |
| Investments | Investment categories, concentrations |
| Risks and Uncertainties | Market risk, concentration risk |
| Plan Amendments | Changes during year |
| Related Party Transactions | Party-in-interest transactions |
| Tax Status | Determination letter, pending application |
| Plan Termination | If applicable |
| Subsequent Events | Material events after year-end |

### Sample Note Structure

```
Note 1 - Description of Plan
Note 2 - Summary of Significant Accounting Policies
Note 3 - Fair Value Measurements
Note 4 - Investments
Note 5 - Party-in-Interest Transactions
Note 6 - Plan Tax Status
Note 7 - Risks and Uncertainties
Note 8 - Exempt Party-in-Interest Transactions
Note 9 - Reconciliation to Form 5500
Note 10 - Subsequent Events
```

## Management Letter

### Purpose

Communicates matters not significant enough for the audit report but worthy of management attention:
- Lesser control deficiencies
- Best practice recommendations
- Efficiency suggestions
- Compliance observations

### Content

| Section | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Introduction | Purpose and context |
| Observations | Issues identified |
| Recommendations | Suggested improvements |
| Management Response | (Optional) Actions planned |

### Sample Observation

```
Observation: Beneficiary Designation Records

During our audit, we noted that beneficiary designation forms were 
not on file for approximately 15% of participants.

Recommendation: We recommend the Plan Administrator implement a 
process to obtain beneficiary designations from all participants 
and periodically remind participants to review and update their 
designations, particularly after life events such as marriage, 
divorce, or birth of children.

Management Response: Management agrees and will implement an 
annual beneficiary designation review process beginning in Q1 20XX.
```

## Form 5500 Integration

### Attachment to Form 5500

The audit report must be attached to Form 5500:
- As Schedule H attachment (Part IV)
- Including financial statements
- Including supplemental schedules
- Electronic filing through EFAST2

### Consistency Requirements

Financial statement amounts must agree with Form 5500:
- Total plan assets
- Total liabilities
- Net assets
- Contributions
- Distributions
- Income

### Reconciliation

If differences exist between Form 5500 and audited financial statements, explain in notes:

```
Note X - Reconciliation of Financial Statements to Form 5500

The following is a reconciliation of net assets available for 
benefits per the financial statements to the Form 5500:

Net assets per financial statements        $XX,XXX,XXX
Adjustment from fair value to contract 
  value for stable value fund                  (XX,XXX)
Net assets per Form 5500                    $XX,XXX,XXX
```

## Report Distribution

### Required Distribution

| Recipient | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| DOL | Via Form 5500 filing |
| IRS | Via Form 5500 filing |
| PBGC | If applicable |
| Plan Committee | For review and approval |

### Available Upon Request

Must be available to:
- Participants
- Beneficiaries
- DOL upon request
- IRS upon request

### Summary Annual Report (SAR)

After Form 5500 filing, distribute SAR to participants:
- Within 9 months of plan year end (or 2 months after extended filing)
- Summarizes financial information
- References availability of full report
