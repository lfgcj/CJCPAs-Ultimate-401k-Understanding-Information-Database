# DOL Regulations for 401(k) Plans

## Overview

The Department of Labor (DOL) is one of the primary federal agencies responsible for regulating 401(k) plans under the Employee Retirement Income Security Act (ERISA). The DOL focuses on protecting participant rights, ensuring fiduciary accountability, and requiring transparent disclosure. This comprehensive guide covers all major DOL regulations affecting 401(k) plans.

## DOL Regulatory Authority

### Statutory Basis

The DOL derives authority from:

| Source | Scope |
|--------|-------|
| ERISA Title I | Reporting, disclosure, fiduciary |
| Reorganization Plan No. 4 | Shared authority with IRS |
| Federal Procedure | Regulatory rulemaking |
| Interpretive Authority | Guidance, advisory opinions |

### DOL Agencies

| Agency | Responsibility |
|--------|----------------|
| Employee Benefits Security Administration (EBSA) | Primary enforcement and guidance |
| Office of Regulations and Interpretations | Regulatory development |
| Office of Exemption Determinations | Prohibited transaction exemptions |
| Office of Enforcement | Investigations and civil actions |

### Regulatory Process

```
Notice of Proposed Rulemaking (NPRM)
         ↓
Public Comment Period (typically 60-90 days)
         ↓
Review of Comments
         ↓
Final Rule Published
         ↓
Effective Date (typically 60+ days)
         ↓
Enforcement Begins
```

## Reporting and Disclosure Requirements

### Form 5500 Annual Report

#### Filing Requirements

| Plan Type | Form | Threshold |
|-----------|------|-----------|
| Large plans | 5500 | 100+ participants |
| Small plans | 5500-SF | <100 participants |
| One-participant plans | 5500-EZ | No employees |

#### Filing Deadline

| Deadline | Date |
|----------|------|
| Regular | 7 months after plan year end |
| Extension | 2.5 additional months with Form 5558 |
| Calendar year | July 31 (October 15 extended) |

#### Required Schedules

| Schedule | Content | When Required |
|----------|---------|---------------|
| Schedule C | Service provider information | Large plans |
| Schedule H | Financial information | Large plans |
| Schedule I | Financial information | Small plans |
| Schedule R | Retirement plan information | All plans |
| Schedule SB | Single employer DB | DB plans only |

### Summary Plan Description (SPD)

#### Required Content

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Plan name and type | Official name and 401(k) type |
| Plan sponsor | Employer name, address, EIN |
| Plan administrator | Contact information |
| Agent for service of process | Legal service contact |
| Eligibility requirements | How to become participant |
| Contribution provisions | How contributions work |
| Vesting schedule | When benefits become owned |
| Benefits provided | What plan provides |
| Claim procedures | How to file benefit claims |
| Statement of ERISA rights | Required language |

#### Distribution Requirements

| Event | Timing |
|-------|--------|
| New participant | Within 90 days of eligibility |
| Material modification | 210 days after plan year of change |
| Request | Within 30 days |
| Updated SPD | Every 5 years (if changed) or 10 years |

### Summary Annual Report (SAR)

Must be distributed:
- Within 9 months of plan year end
- Or 2 months after Form 5500 due date (extended)
- To all participants and beneficiaries receiving benefits

### Individual Benefit Statements

#### Participant-Directed Plans

| Requirement | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Account balance | Quarterly |
| Investment allocation | Quarterly |
| Investment information | Quarterly |
| Fee disclosure | Quarterly |

#### Non-Participant-Directed Plans

| Requirement | Frequency |
|-------------|-----------|
| Account balance | Annually |
| Vested percentage | Annually |

### Electronic Disclosure

DOL allows electronic delivery if:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Affirmative consent | Participant agrees to electronic delivery |
| Notice | Participant informed of right to paper |
| Access | Participant has effective access |
| Hardware/software | Participant has necessary technology |
| Withdrawal | Participant can revoke consent |

## Fiduciary Regulations

### Fiduciary Definition (29 CFR 2510.3-21)

A person is a fiduciary if they:

| Function | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Discretionary authority | Control over plan management |
| Asset control | Authority over plan assets |
| Administrative discretion | Discretionary administration responsibility |
| Investment advice | Provides advice for fee |

### Investment Advice Definition

Under the current rule, investment advice includes:

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|-------------|
| Recommendation | Regarding investment |
| For fee | Direct or indirect compensation |
| Individualized | Based on particular needs |
| Primary basis | Advice will be primary basis for decision |
| Regular basis | Advice provided regularly |

### Fiduciary Duties (29 CFR 2550)

#### Duty of Loyalty

Act solely in interest of participants:
- No self-dealing
- No conflicts of interest
- Exclusive purpose rule

#### Duty of Prudence

Prudent expert standard:
- Care, skill, prudence, diligence
- Person familiar with such matters
- Under prevailing circumstances

#### Duty to Diversify

Minimize risk of large losses:
- Diversify investments
- Unless clearly prudent not to
- Consider correlation of investments

#### Duty to Follow Documents

Follow plan documents:
- Unless inconsistent with ERISA
- Plan terms control
- Cannot override ERISA

### ERISA Section 404(c) Protection

For participant-directed plans:

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Control | Participants must have control |
| Information | Sufficient information provided |
| Options | At least 3 diversified choices |
| Transfer frequency | At least quarterly |
| Diversification | Opportunity to diversify |

When requirements met, fiduciary not liable for participant investment decisions.

### Qualified Default Investment Alternative (QDIA)

#### Approved QDIA Types

| Type | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Target-date fund | Based on retirement date |
| Balanced fund | Mix of equity and fixed income |
| Managed account | Professionally managed |
| Capital preservation | First 120 days only |

#### QDIA Requirements

| Requirement | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Diversified | Not concentrated in employer securities |
| Asset-based | Based on participant data |
| Professional management | Managed by investment professional |
| Notice | Participants informed annually |

## Prohibited Transaction Rules

### ERISA Section 406 Prohibitions

#### Party in Interest Transactions (406(a))

A fiduciary cannot cause plan to:

| Transaction | Prohibition |
|-------------|-------------|
| Sale/exchange | Sell or exchange property |
| Lending | Lend money or extend credit |
| Furnishing services | Provide goods/services for more than reasonable compensation |
| Transfer assets | Transfer plan assets |
| Use assets | Use assets for benefit of party in interest |

#### Self-Dealing (406(b))

A fiduciary cannot:

| Prohibition | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Self-interest | Deal with assets for own interest |
| Adverse party | Act on behalf of adverse party |
| Consideration | Receive consideration from transaction party |

### Parties in Interest

Include:

| Category | Examples |
|----------|----------|
| Fiduciaries | Trustees, plan administrators |
| Service providers | Recordkeepers, TPAs, advisors |
| Employer | Plan sponsor and affiliates |
| Employee organizations | Unions |
| Owners | 10%+ shareholders |
| Family members | Spouses, children of above |

### Statutory Exemptions (ERISA 408)

| Exemption | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Participant loans | If meet IRC 72(p) requirements |
| Reasonable compensation | For necessary services |
| Office space | At reasonable cost |
| Ancillary services | By bank/insurance company |
| Investment advice | Under PPA 2006 rules |

### Class Exemptions

DOL-issued exemptions for common transactions:

| Exemption | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| PTE 84-14 | QPAM transactions |
| PTE 91-38 | Bank collective investment funds |
| PTE 95-60 | Insurance company separate accounts |
| PTE 96-23 | In-house asset managers |
| PTE 2020-02 | Investment advice |

### Prohibited Transaction Consequences

| Consequence | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| 15% excise tax | IRC Section 4975 |
| Correction | Transaction must be undone |
| 100% excise tax | If not corrected |
| Fiduciary liability | Personal liability possible |
| Criminal penalties | For willful violations |

## Fee Disclosure Requirements

### 408(b)(2) Service Provider Disclosure

#### Covered Service Providers

Providers who:
- Expect $1,000+ in compensation
- Provide fiduciary, registered investment advisory, recordkeeping, brokerage, or certain other services

#### Required Disclosures

| Category | Information |
|----------|-------------|
| Services | Description of all services |
| Status | Fiduciary or RIA status |
| Direct compensation | Fees paid by plan |
| Indirect compensation | Fees from third parties |
| Investment information | Expenses, fees, restrictions |
| Manner of compensation | How compensation received |

#### Timing

| Event | Deadline |
|-------|----------|
| Initial contract | Before entering agreement |
| Changes | Within 60 days of change |
| Annual | Reasonable advance notice of changes |
| Request | As soon as practicable |

### 404(a)-5 Participant Fee Disclosure

#### Annual Disclosure Requirements

Must disclose:

| Category | Information |
|----------|-------------|
| General plan information | How to give instructions |
| Administrative fees | Fees charged to accounts |
| Individual fees | Transaction-based fees |
| Investment information | For each investment option |

#### Investment Information Required

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Name and type | Investment name and category |
| Performance | 1, 5, 10-year returns |
| Benchmark | Appropriate benchmark comparison |
| Fees and expenses | Total annual operating expenses |
| Website | DOL website for additional information |

#### Quarterly Statement Requirements

Must include:

| Element | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Account balance | Beginning and ending |
| Dollar amount of fees | Fees charged during quarter |
| Description of services | What fees covered |

## Plan Asset Regulations

### What Constitutes Plan Assets (29 CFR 2510.3-101)

#### General Rule

When plan invests in entity, entity's underlying assets may be plan assets if:
- Plan owns equity interest
- Entity is not operating company
- No exception applies

#### Look-Through Rule

Plan assets include:
- Direct plan holdings
- Undivided interest in underlying assets
- When significant participation exists

#### Exceptions

Plan assets do NOT include underlying assets when:

| Exception | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Publicly traded | Securities traded on exchange |
| Operating company | Entity operating business |
| <25% participation | Benefit plan investors own <25% |
| Registered investment company | Mutual funds |
| Bank collective trusts | Certain bank products |
| Insurance company products | Certain insurance products |

### Employer Securities and Real Property

#### Limits

| Asset Type | Maximum |
|------------|---------|
| Qualifying employer securities | 10% of fair market value |
| Qualifying employer real property | 10% of fair market value |
| Combined | 10% total |

#### Eligible Individual Account Plans

Some plans exempt from limits:
- Profit sharing plans
- Stock bonus plans
- ESOPs (special rules)

#### Qualifying Requirements

Employer securities must be:
- Stock or marketable obligation
- Issued by employer or affiliate
- Meet specific requirements

## Enforcement and Penalties

### DOL Enforcement Authority

| Authority | Scope |
|-----------|-------|
| Investigation | Examine records, interview |
| Subpoena | Compel production |
| Civil action | Sue in federal court |
| Criminal referral | Refer to DOJ |

### Civil Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Late Form 5500 | $250/day, up to $150,000 |
| Failure to provide documents | Up to $110/day per participant |
| Failure to provide information | Up to $100/day to DOL |
| ERISA Section 502(c)(7) | Blackout notice failures |

### Criminal Penalties

| Violation | Penalty |
|-----------|---------|
| Willful violation | Up to $100,000 fine and/or 10 years |
| Organization | Up to $500,000 fine |
| Making false statements | Separate penalties |

### Common Enforcement Issues

| Issue | DOL Focus |
|-------|-----------|
| Late contributions | Timely deposit requirement |
| Fee reasonableness | Excessive fees |
| Self-dealing | Prohibited transactions |
| Missing participants | Locating lost participants |
| Cybersecurity | Data protection failures |

## Voluntary Correction Programs

### Delinquent Filer Voluntary Compliance Program (DFVCP)

For late Form 5500 filings:

| Plan Size | Penalty per Year |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Small plan | $10 per day, max $750/year |
| Large plan | $10 per day, max $2,000/year |
| Maximum total | $4,000 (small) / $4,000 (large) |

### Voluntary Fiduciary Correction Program (VFCP)

For operational violations:

| Covered Violation | Correction |
|-------------------|------------|
| Late contributions | Deposit with lost earnings |
| Loan failures | Correct with lost earnings |
| Participant loans as PT | Review and correct |
| Expense payments | Restore improper payments |
| Sales to parties in interest | Undo transaction |

#### VFCP Process

```
Step 1: Self-identify violation
Step 2: Calculate correction
Step 3: Make correction
Step 4: Submit application to DOL
Step 5: Receive no-action letter
```

#### Lost Earnings Calculator

DOL provides online calculator:
- Enter principal amount
- Enter loss date and recovery date
- Uses IRS underpayment rates
- Calculates required restoration

### Prohibited Transaction Exemption Program

Request individual exemption for specific transaction:
- File application with EBSA
- Pay filing fee
- Demonstrate protective conditions
- Public comment period
- DOL issues exemption or denial

## Cybersecurity Guidance

### DOL Cybersecurity Best Practices (2021)

#### For Plan Sponsors

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Document program | Formal cybersecurity program |
| Annual assessment | Review risks and controls |
| Third-party audit | Independent security assessment |
| Clear roles | Define cybersecurity responsibilities |
| Data protection | Properly protect plan data |

#### For Service Providers

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Security standards | Follow industry standards (SOC 2) |
| Incident response | Have response procedures |
| Business continuity | Maintain backup and recovery |
| Insurance | Carry adequate cyber coverage |
| Annual reports | Provide security attestations |

#### For Participants

| Practice | Description |
|----------|-------------|
| Strong passwords | Use complex, unique passwords |
| Multi-factor authentication | Enable when available |
| Monitor accounts | Review statements regularly |
| Secure access | Use secure networks |
| Update information | Keep contact information current |

### Cybersecurity Questionnaire

DOL expects plan sponsors to ask service providers:

| Topic | Questions |
|-------|-----------|
| Security program | Is there a formal program? |
| Standards | Which security standards followed? |
| Audits | Are independent audits performed? |
| Incidents | History of breaches? Response procedures? |
| Insurance | What cyber coverage is in place? |
| Encryption | How is data protected? |

## Recent Regulatory Developments

### ESG Investing Rule (2022)

DOL guidance on environmental, social, governance factors:

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Pecuniary factors | Financial factors must drive decisions |
| ESG consideration | May consider if financially relevant |
| Tiebreaker | Can use as tiebreaker between equivalent options |
| Documentation | Document decision-making process |

### Retirement Security Rule (2024)

Expanded fiduciary definition for investment advice:
- Broader definition of investment advice
- One-time advice may be fiduciary
- Rollover advice covered
- PTE 2020-02 compliance required

### Missing Participants Guidance

DOL guidance on locating missing participants:

| Step | Description |
|------|-------------|
| Certified mail | Use certified mail to last address |
| Electronic search | Search free electronic databases |
| Letter forwarding | Use commercial locator |
| Social media | Search social media |
| Plan beneficiaries | Contact designated beneficiaries |

### Mental Health Parity

Though primarily health plan focused, aspects may affect 401(k):
- Integrated benefit administration
- Compliance coordination
- Disclosure requirements
